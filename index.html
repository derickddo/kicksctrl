What we do

We built the recovery chain footwear was missing

A shoe is rubber, foam, textile, and plastic bonded together — impossible to recycle through ordinary single-stream systems. We separate it, and route each material back into use.

01

Collect

We aggregate post-consumer footwear from brand take-back, retail, and municipal waste streams.

02

Recover

Return

About us

Building the missing layer of the footwear supply chain

Footwear poses a huge, overlooked waste challenge. The staggering volumes produced each year — with less than 5% ever recycled — point to a looming problem hiding in plain sight.

We approach it from a design perspective: closing the loop from where the most research and technology already exists to the end of the waste stream, where almost none does. We believe this underresearched problem space is where the future will be decided.

True shoe-to-shoe circularity is only possible through perseverance, resilience, and creativity — and this venture is currently part of Carbon13 Cohort 6.

Infrastructure first

We build physical capacity, not campaigns. Throughput is the product.

Material purity

Output is graded to spec, so it competes with virgin feedstock — not just diverts it.

Auditable chain

Every tonne is tracked from intake to offtake for compliance-grade reporting.

Modular scale

Facilities replicate as standardized units, so capacity grows predictably.
